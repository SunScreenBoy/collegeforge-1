Advice


"Don't procrastinate" seems like pretty good advice to me. Start writing early so that when you give your essays to other people to read, you can write different ones if you don't like the ones you had or you think you can't expand on a topic you've already written. When I was doing the essays I wrote almost 8 different essays. I guess I should say to prepare different topics to write about? Some ideas work better than others. Find friends that aren't afraid to tell you their true opinion.

UCs only require 2 essays, so the two topics I wrote about were my cultural background/possible future ambitions and my experience as a leader in Firebird Youth Chinese Orchestra.

In school: varsity swim for one year, on swim team for 2 years, officer of jam and youth alive clubs for 1 year each, active in Reuse club for 1 year
Out of school: Chinese school TA for 1 year, total of 8 years in a Chinese youth orchestra, volunteering at day camps

AP Chinese, AP Euro, AP American History, AP Language and Composition, AP Biology

UC Davis, Cal Poly SLO, UCSC, UCSB, San Jose State, and some other CSUs

waitlisted at UCSD, rejected from: UC Berkeley, UCLA, UC Irvine

UC Irvine was being weird this year. According to other parents, they're also saying that Irvine is being weirdly selective because there's no way I got into Davis but not Irvine. There was a kid that got into Berkeley but rejected from Irvine.
If you think you can go without it, don't hire a professional essay reader/editor. Unless you think your writing skills are very poor, don't get someone else to rewrite your essays for you.
